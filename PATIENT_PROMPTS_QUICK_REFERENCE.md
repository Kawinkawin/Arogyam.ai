## 🫀 CARDIOLOGIST - Use This Template

```
I'm a [AGE]-year-old [MALE/FEMALE]. I've been experiencing [CHEST PAIN/PALPITATIONS/SHORTNESS OF BREATH] for [DURATION].

SYMPTOM DETAILS:
- Location: [describe exactly where]
- Feeling: [sharp/pressure/dull/burning/fluttering]
- Severity: [1-10]
- When: [during activity/at rest/at night/specific time]
- Triggers: [what makes it worse?]
- Relief: [what helps?]
- Other symptoms: [sweating/nausea/fatigue?]

MEDICAL HISTORY:
[Any prior heart problems, high blood pressure, diabetes, etc. or "No prior heart issues"]

MEDICATIONS:
[List all medications you take with doses, or "Not taking any medications"]

FAMILY HISTORY:
[Father/mother had heart disease, high cholesterol, or stroke? Or "No known family history"]

RISK FACTORS:
- Smoking: [yes/no/quit X years ago]
- Exercise: [sedentary/moderate/active, how often?]
- Diet: [describe briefly]
- Weight: [describe]

WHAT I'VE TRIED:
[Any treatments you've done? Or "Nothing yet"]

IMAGING/TESTS:
[Do you have chest X-ray, EKG, or CT? Include if available]

MY MAIN CONCERN:
[What worries you most? Heart attack risk? Urgent?]

SPECIFIC QUESTION:
"Is this serious? Do I need to go to the ER? What condition could this be?"
```

I'm a 58-year-old male. For 3 days, I've been having chest pressure - about 6/10 severity.

- Exercise: Very sedentary, desk job, minimal activity

WHAT I'VE TRIED:
Tried resting more, avoided stairs, but it's still happening

IMAGING/TESTS:
Had EKG done yesterday - doctor said "borderline finding" and wants me to follow up

MY MAIN CONCERN:
I'm terrified I'm having angina or about to have a heart attack. My timing is scary given my age and my father's history.

## ⚡ QUICK TIPS FOR BEST RESULTS

1. **BE SPECIFIC, NOT VAGUE**
   - Bad: "I have pain"
   - Good: "I have sharp pain on the inside of my left knee when going down stairs"

2. **INCLUDE TIMELINE**
   - Bad: "For a while"
   - Good: "For 6 weeks, specifically since March 15th"

3. **DESCRIBE HOW IT AFFECTS YOU**
   - Bad: "I feel bad"
   - Good: "I can't walk normally, I'm limping, and I can't exercise"

4. **INCLUDE WHAT YOU'VE TRIED**
   - Bad: "Nothing helps"
   - Good: "I tried ibuprofen 600mg twice daily for 2 weeks - no improvement. I tried ice for 20 min - temporary relief only"

5. **ATTACH IMAGES WHEN RELEVANT**
   - Photos of rashes (with ruler for scale)
   - Lab results
   - X-rays or CT scans
   - Chart of how symptoms change over time

6. **ASK SPECIFIC QUESTIONS**
   - Bad: "What's wrong with me?"
   - Good: "Based on my symptoms and imaging, what are the top 3 diagnoses you'd consider? Do I need surgery? What's the recovery timeline?"
