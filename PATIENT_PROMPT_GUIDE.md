# 👨‍⚕️ Optimal Patient Prompts for AI Doctor System

## How to Get the Best Responses from Your AI Specialists

As a patient, using **structured, detailed prompts** will get you the most helpful medical analysis. Here are professional examples for each specialist.

---

## 🫀 CARDIOLOGIST - Sample Patient Prompts

### Prompt 1: Chest Pain with Risk Factors
```
"I'm a 55-year-old male with a 10-year history of hypertension and type 2 diabetes. 
For the past 3 days, I've been experiencing intermittent chest discomfort - a pressure 
sensation in the center of my chest, especially when climbing stairs or walking uphill. 

The pain lasts about 5-10 minutes and goes away with rest. I've never had this before. 
I'm on metoprolol and lisinopril for my blood pressure, and I also take atorvastatin 
for cholesterol.

I haven't had a heart attack, but my father had one at age 60.

I've attached a recent chest CT scan from 2 days ago. Can you review it and let me know 
what you see? Should I be worried?"

KEY ELEMENTS:
✓ Age and gender
✓ Risk factors (HTN, diabetes)
✓ Specific symptom characteristics (location, duration, trigger, relief)
✓ Timeline (when it started)
✓ Medications
✓ Family history
✓ Image uploaded (CT, X-ray, or ECG if available)
✓ Specific question asked
```

### Prompt 2: Palpitations and Shortness of Breath
```
"I'm a 42-year-old woman and for the last 2 weeks, I've been having episodes of 
heart palpitations - I can feel my heart racing or skipping beats, especially in 
the evening after coffee. 

I've also been more short of breath than usual, even doing normal activities like 
housework. No chest pain, but I feel anxious during these episodes.

I have no history of heart problems, and I'm not on any medications. I've been stressed 
at work lately due to a big project deadline.

My grandmother had atrial fibrillation when she was older. I've uploaded a recent 
chest X-ray and if you need it, I can get an EKG. What could this be?"

KEY ELEMENTS:
✓ Age and sex
✓ Symptom details (frequency, triggers, timing)
✓ Associated symptoms
✓ Medical history (none/specific)
✓ Current medications (none/specific)
✓ Family history
✓ Stress/lifestyle context
✓ Image available
✓ Willingness to do more tests
```

### Prompt 3: Post-Surgery Follow-up
```
"I had bypass surgery 4 weeks ago (triple bypass). I've been recovering well and 
doing my cardiac rehabilitation program 3x per week.

My surgical scars are healing well - still a bit tender but no redness or discharge. 
I can walk about 15 minutes now, and my shortness of breath has improved a lot.

However, I occasionally feel some sharp chest pain when I twist or cough - different 
from my angina pain before surgery. Also, my ankles have been slightly swollen for the 
past week.

I'm on aspirin, beta blocker, ACE inhibitor, and a statin. My surgical team said to 
check in if anything felt different. I've included my post-op chest CT scan. Is this 
swelling normal? Should I be concerned?"

KEY ELEMENTS:
✓ Recent surgery details
✓ Timeline of recovery
✓ Specific questions about new symptoms
✓ Rehabilitation progress
✓ Current medications
✓ Wound healing status
✓ Images included
✓ When to worry context
```

## 🧠 NEUROLOGIST - Sample Patient Prompts

I tried different over-the-counter pain relievers, but only prescription triptans 
help. My mother and her sister both get similar headaches. I've had a recent brain MRI

KEY ELEMENTS:
✓ Age, sex, detailed timeline
✓ Headache characteristics (location, quality, duration)
✓ Associated symptoms (visual, nausea)
✓ Trigger/prodrome
✓ Medication trials
✓ Family history
✓ Recent imaging (MRI) - normal

I've had a recent brain MRI which my regular doctor said looked normal. I included the

## 🦴 ORTHOPEDIC SURGEON - Sample Patient Prompts

### Prompt 1: Acute Knee Injury
```
"I'm a 34-year-old male. Yesterday during a basketball game, I twisted my left knee 
while making a sharp cut. I heard a 'pop' sound and immediately fell.

Right now: My knee is very swollen - it swelled up within an hour of the injury. 
I can barely walk and I'm using crutches. The pain is severe, especially at the 
inside of the knee. I can't straighten my leg all the way, and when I try to move 
it, there's a sharp catching sensation.

I've iced it and elevated it overnight, and I took ibuprofen, but it's not helping much. 
I'm worried I might have torn something.

No prior knee injuries. I'm otherwise healthy and active (play sports 2-3x per week).

I've had X-rays and an MRI done today. The X-rays look normal, but I need your expert 
review of the images. What do you think is the injury? Do I need surgery?"

KEY ELEMENTS:
✓ Age and mechanism of injury (specific)
✓ Symptoms at time of injury (pop sensation)
✓ Current findings (swelling, pain, loss of motion, catching)
✓ Timeline
✓ Conservative treatment already tried
✓ Functional limitation
✓ Medical history
✓ Imaging obtained
✓ Specific clinical question
```

KEY ELEMENTS:
✓ Age and location of pain
✓ Onset (gradual, no trauma)

✓ Medications/medical history
✓ Imaging obtained

✓ Skin type and sun history
✓ Family history
✓ Number of moles
✓ Patient's specific concern (skin cancer?)

✓ Specific ABCDE elements

## 📋 UNIVERSAL BEST PRACTICES FOR ALL PROMPTS

### ✅ DO Include:
1. **Basic Demographics**: Age, sex (relevant for many conditions)
2. **Specific Timeline**: "for 2 weeks" NOT "for a while"
3. **Detailed Symptom Description**:
   - WHERE (location)?
   - WHAT (quality - sharp, dull, pressure, burning)?
   - WHEN (frequency, time of day)?
   - HOW LONG (duration)?
   - WHAT MAKES IT WORSE/BETTER?
4. **Associated Symptoms**: What else is happening?
5. **Medical History**: Relevant past medical conditions
6. **Current Medications**: ALL of them with doses if possible
7. **Family History**: Relevant to the condition
8. **Lifestyle/Risk Factors**: Smoking, alcohol, occupation, activity level
9. **Recent Testing**: Labs, imaging already done
10. **What You've Already Tried**: Treatments, medications
11. **Specific Question**: What do you want to know?
12. **Images**: Attach when relevant (X-rays, photos of skin, etc.)
13. **Timeline of Changes**: How it started, how it's progressed
14. **Impact on Life**: How is it affecting you?

### ❌ DON'T Include:
- Vague timeframes ("lately," "for a while")
- Vague symptoms ("not feeling well," "something's wrong")
- Incomplete information on medications
- Too much irrelevant information
- Emotional language instead of symptom description
- Self-diagnosis ("I think it's cancer/heart disease")
- Multiple unrelated complaints without connection

## 🎯 PROMPT STRUCTURE TEMPLATE

Use this structure for ANY condition to get the best response:

```
"I'm a [AGE]-year-old [GENDER]. 

MAIN COMPLAINT:
[Specific symptom with timeline - e.g., "chest pain for 3 days"]

DETAILED SYMPTOM DESCRIPTION:
- Location: [specific]
- Character: [how does it feel?]
- Severity: [1-10 scale]
- Timing: [when does it happen?]
- Triggers: [what makes it worse?]
- Relief: [what makes it better?]
- Associated symptoms: [what else?]

MEDICAL HISTORY:
[List relevant conditions]

MEDICATIONS:
[All current medications with doses]

FAMILY HISTORY:
[Relevant to this problem]

RISK FACTORS/LIFESTYLE:
[Smoking, alcohol, occupation, activity level]

RECENT TESTING:
[Labs, imaging, any results]

WHAT I'VE TRIED:
[Previous treatments/medications and results]

SPECIFIC CONCERNS:
[What worries you most?]

MY QUESTION:
[What do you want to know? What should I do?]

[IMAGES ATTACHED if relevant]"
```

## 💡 EXAMPLE: How a GOOD Prompt Gets GOOD Responses

### Poor Prompt (Generic):
```
"I have back pain. It's really bad. I don't know what's wrong. 
Should I go to the hospital? I'm worried. Help me."
```
**Response Will Be**: Generic, can't determine urgency, will suggest seeing doctor

---

### Excellent Prompt (Specific):
```
"I'm a 45-year-old male. For 3 days, I've had severe lower back pain - 
worst pain of my life (9/10).

Symptom details:
- Started suddenly while lifting a box at work
- Pain is in lower left back, radiates down left leg to knee
- Constant aching, worse with any bending forward or twisting
- Numbness/tingling in my left foot (new today - very worried)
- Pain is NOT better with rest or ibuprofen
- I can barely walk

Medical history: Three prior episodes of back pain (2015, 2018, 2021) 
that resolved with rest and PT.

Medications: Ibuprofen 600mg, haven't helped

Red flags I'm worried about: 
- Numbness spreading
- I can't move my left foot as well as usual
- Loss of bowel control - haven't had an issue yet but I'm panicked 
  about this based on what I read online

Imaging: Got lumbar spine X-rays done - I included them.

Question: Do I need an MRI? Could this be a herniated disc? 
Is there nerve damage? How urgent is this - should I go to ER?"
```

**Response Will Be**: Medical-laser-focused, can assess urgency of neuro symptoms, 
specific MRI recommendation, differential diagnosis, clear next steps

---

## 🎁 BONUS: Questions That Get Doctor to Give BEST Analysis

Ask at the END of your prompt:

✅ **"Based on my symptoms and the images, what are the top 3 diagnoses you're thinking?"**
✅ **"How urgent is this - is it an emergency or routine?"**
✅ **"What should I do next - do I need more testing, a specialist, medication, or something else?"**
✅ **"What are the red flags I should watch for?"**
✅ **"Is this something I can manage at home or do I need to see someone?"**
✅ **"What's the likely timeline - will this go away on its own or do I need treatment?"**

## 📊 COMPARISON: By Adding Detail Specificity

### ❌ BEFORE (Vague):
```
"I've been sick for a while and I'm tired. What's wrong with me?"
```
**AI Response**: Generic, unhelpful, suggests seeing doctor

### ✅ AFTER (Specific):
```
"I'm a 52-year-old male. For exactly 6 weeks, I've had persistent 
fatigue - I wake up tired after 8 hours sleep, which is new for me.

Associated:
- Low-grade fevers (99-100°F) in afternoons, no fever at night
- Night sweats - soaking my shirt, worst night 1-2x per week
- Lost 7 pounds unintentionally in 6 weeks
- No cough, no sore throat, no respiratory symptoms
- Mild headaches (days 3 and 5 of each week)
- Appetite normal

No travels, no sick contacts, fully vaccinated.

History: Never had anything like this. Generally healthy except treated 
hypertension (well controlled on one medication).

Imaging: Chest X-ray 1 week ago was normal.

Labs (1 week ago): CBC normal, CMP normal, TSH normal.

Concerned about: Infection, cancer, or thyroid issue despite normal TSH.

Question: What could cause unexplained fever + night sweats + weight loss 
for 6 weeks with normal labs and chest X-ray? What tests do I need?"
```
**AI Response**: Differential diagnosis with specific conditions to consider, 
specific additional testing recommendations, urgency assessment

---

**TIP**: The more specific and detailed you are, the more specific and helpful 
the AI doctor's response will be!
